Why do startups get all the love?

We only give ambitious entrepreneurs one path: start a startup.

One track mind

Unicorn folklore and venture capital hype make founder mode feel like the only way to be your own boss. Not everyone wants to build a product. Many want huge impact and autonomy.

Legacy businesses are dying

Owner operators are retiring. Many firms could be saved, yet they are replaced by new startups that adopt modern tools. Save the jobs and cut switching costs.

Career switches are hard

Future founders get a sea of support from accelerators and courses. The quit your job leap is high risk and high reward. Entrepreneurs who want to lead turnarounds deserve the same pathway.

Sustainable profit is not sexy

Venture funding seeks hockey sticks by design. Sustainable profit often clashes with that model. Entrepreneurs can build real wealth by fixing service firms. We need a new model to finance it.

Embed a vetted operator with a one year mandate and clear guardrails.

Vet and match

A curated pool of operators matched to owners who offer real authority and a clean mandate.

Prep and plan

A three month cohort focuses on diagnostics, cash clarity, throughput, and alignment. The output is a first 30-60-90 everyone can understand.

Execute and show wins

Weekly metrics, tight scope, and a shipping cadence. Confidence returns when work is visible and useful.

A repeatable program that aligns incentives and protects the core business.

Guardrails

Clear decision rights. Explicit constraints. Review cadence with advisors who have shipped real programs. Owners get line of sight without micromanagement.

Aligned upside

Operator incentives tie to durable cash flow and employee outcomes. The north star is simple. Cash positive and fair distribution of gains.

Lightweight tools

Dashboards that track leading indicators. Operating rhythms that fit on one page. Process that helps people do their work, not fight software.

Nonprofit backbone

We are building an institution with mission first design. The objective is healthy companies that treat people well. The model follows that truth.

What this does at scale

Local economies

Stabilized mid sized firms keep skills, apprenticeships, and supplier networks alive. These are the quiet engines of a region.

Better managers

Programs that teach constraint setting and throughput create leaders who build teams that ship. That culture persists after the cohort ends.

Fair distribution

When cash improves, employees benefit. That is the agreement. It is visible and simple to audit.

Less waste

Fewer me too products. More repair and renewal of useful businesses. Capital gets more productive in the places where people work.
